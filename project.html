  15. Present, in your own words, an overview of your first choice organization - who is the community they serve and what services do they provide? (300-500 words. DO NOT copy paste directly from their site). *

    The Midwest Food Bank helps alleviate hunger and poverty by assisting the sourcing and distribution food donations to pantries in need, nonprofits and disaster relief. One of their primary goals is to reliably source food donations from places where it would simply be thrown out. They aim to eliminate waste and give it to the people that need it the most. They have facilities in Bloomington, Peoria and Morton IL, Indianapolis IN, Peachtree City GA, Fort Myers FL, Gilbert AZ, East Africa, and Haiti. They have served over 275 counties across the U.S., as well as extensive efforts in East Africa and Haiti.

    One of their goals is to provide this service without a cost to the food donors or those receiving the food. They help to work with partners such as Caterpillar, Salvation Army, State Farm, and many others. Their partners assist them in being able to receive a large amount of donations, as well as those who help in transporting and circulating food to the areas in need. After hurricane Katrina hit in 2005, the Midwest Food Bank partnered with Salvation Army. They have worked in Houston, Puerto Rico, and are currently aiding disaster relief efforts from hurricane Florence. In their efforts for the Carolinas, they are providing enough food for families of 4 for about 4 days, as well as common toiletries needed. The Midwest Food Bank has their own fleet of semi-trucks that are used for distribution, and volunteers work to organize and pack supplies before they are shipped to the partners they work with. Salvation Army states on their website that as of late 2017, both organizations have partnered together to deliver over 275,000 boxes of food for disaster areas. Globally, they have worked with missionaries, clinics, schools, and other organizations in almost 20 countries. They are currently working in Kenya, Africa.

    Source: http://disaster.salvationarmyusa.org/news/2017/09/13/the-salvation-army-and-midwest-food-bank-partner-to-serve-hurricane-survivors
